The Identity of The Batman

The Batman is an alias for the fictional billionaire Bruce Wayne. However, the Batman hasn't always been Bruce Wayne, as the cape and cowl have been worn by his successors, such as his adopted sons Richard Grayson and Jason Todd, his biological son Damian Wayne, or by acquaintances who joined the Bat Family, such as Tim Drake. Nevertheless, Bruce Wayne is the primary Batman in most iterations of the character. The secret identity of Batman is an important aspect of how he is perceived by villains and criminals on the streets of Gotham. His anonymity provides security and safety for those in his personal life, and it also enhances the influence of fear and intimidation on his foes.

Batman however doesn't have his identity hidden to everyone in his life, there are a few special people who have learned the identity of Batman. A few important people who know his idenetity are:

  • Alfred Pennyworth
  • James Gordon
  • Lucius Fox

Detective Comic's Flagship Character

Batman is the longest running comic series for Detecive Comics. With this he is also considered the flagship character for the company often being put in roles such as the mascot, leader of the Justice League and recieving a higher frequency of attention over other heroes. He shares the flagship role with Superman and Wonderwoman. He is often put in a team of original seven heroes, these heroes are usually the founders of the Justice League.

The Justice League Founders

  1. Batman
  2. Superman
  3. Wonder Woman
  4. The Flash
  5. Green Lantern
  6. Aquaman
  7. Martian Manhunter, Shazam, or Cyborg

Batman's leadership role

Batman along with Superman, and Wonder Woman are considered the leaders of the Justice League. Batman's leadership role in the organization brought his fame and influnce outside of Gotham. With this change in his familiarity Batman needed to change his reputation for a better future. His visage became less menacing and he became more open although he is still secluded compared to his colleagues.
